Week 5

Tasks Completed

CAN Communication Test Plan Development

This week I worked on creating a CAN communication test plan for the elevator project. The goal was to establish a structured method for validating CAN bus communication between the STM32 controllers and documenting expected behavior.

The test plan included communication scenarios, expected message responses, and procedures for verifying successful data transmission across the network.

Team Review of Documentation

After developing the CAN communication test plan, I reviewed the document with team members to gather feedback and ensure the testing procedures were clear and practical.

The review process helped identify areas that required clarification and ensured that everyone understood the testing objectives before implementation.

Elevator Switch Wiring

I physically wired new switches into the elevator system. These switches were installed to support user input and future testing of elevator requests and floor selection functionality.

After installation, I verified the wiring connections and prepared the hardware for integration with the STM32 controllers.

Challenges

One challenge I faced was attempting to capture a reliable CAN signal from the CAN High and CAN Low lines using an oscilloscope. Obtaining a clear waveform required proper probe placement and understanding how differential CAN signals behave during communication.

What I Learned

This week I learned more about backend web development and how PHP can be a strong option for creating dynamic web applications. I also gained a better understanding of CAN communication testing and documentation practices.

  • Continue testing CAN communication within the elevator system.
